The Problem

Good opportunities stall because teams aren't aligned — not because the deal is bad

In most organizations, an opportunity moves through multiple teams before anyone realizes Legal has a concern, Product has a capacity issue, or PR sees a reputational flag. By then it's already bounced through several handoffs and wasted everyone's time.

The problem isn't the opportunity. It's that no one had a shared view of all five perspectives at once — before the process started.

What I Built

One tool. Five perspectives. One clear picture.

Clearpath is a guided, three-screen assessment that evaluates any opportunity — a business loan, a real estate deal, an internal initiative, or a community program — across five organizational lenses simultaneously: Lending, Legal, Product, PR, and Executive Leadership. It produces a full strategic readiness picture in under 5 minutes, simple enough for a small business owner and substantive enough for a Chief Credit Officer.

How It Works

No AI. No black box. Every score is explainable.

Clearpath uses transparent, rules-based logic — the same way a checklist works, just made visual and interactive. Here's the full flow in plain language:

From intake to insight in four steps:

1
You answer 6–8 questions about the opportunity — revenue consistency, repayment confidence, community impact, team capacity. Each answer maps to a point value based on how strongly it signals readiness.
2
Six readiness scores are calculated across Readiness, Risk, Growth Potential, Relationship Value, Deposit Opportunity, and Community Impact. Think of it as a report card — each category gets its own grade derived from your answers.
3
Five departmental stances are generated — Lending, Legal, Product, PR, and Executive Leadership each receive a position (Supportive, Conditional, or Concerns) based on what the scores suggest about where they'd likely land. This is the stakeholder alignment layer.
4
Everything synthesizes into a strategic output — a plain-English recommendation, an overall alignment score, a visual review path, scenario testing, and clear next steps. A Decision Style toggle (Conservative / Balanced / Growth-Oriented) adjusts how scores are weighted throughout.
Scope & Boundaries

What Clearpath does not do — on purpose

The most important design decision was choosing what to leave out. Clearpath surfaces perspectives — it does not make decisions.

⚠️ Clearpath does not replace:

  • 🏦 Underwriting. It identifies whether an opportunity is ready for underwriting — not whether it should be approved.
  • ⚖️ Legal and compliance review. Legal flags are surfaced early, but professional review is always required.
  • 👤 Human decision-makers. Every output includes a "Send for Manual Review" option. People make the final call — always.
  • 🤖 AI or machine learning. Every score is produced by transparent, auditable, rule-based logic. No black box, no automated decisions.
The Bigger Picture

Clearpath is one layer. Senior executives need both.

Clearpath answers: "Is this opportunity ready — and are we aligned to move on it?" But a Chief Credit Officer is also asking: "What does our entire portfolio look like if we keep approving deals like this one?" That's a different question — and a different tool.

Strategic Takeaway

Why this matters beyond the tool

The strongest organizations don't just evaluate opportunities — they evaluate how their own teams will respond to them. Clearpath is built on that idea: surface every perspective early, reduce friction before it compounds, and make sure the right people have the right information before the first meeting happens.

"The strongest systems don't replace good judgment. They make sure good judgment gets the right information at the right time."
